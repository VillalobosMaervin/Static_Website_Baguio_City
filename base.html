THE MANSION

What to expect

The Mansion is the official summer residence of the President of the Philippines. Older than the city itself, it was originally built as the seat of power of Governor Generals during the American Occupation. Its architecture was designed by Architect Daniel Burnham, who was also responsible for the city planning of Baguio and Manila.

What can you do

Discover the treasures of the past as you wander through the mansion's mini-museum, showcasing a curated collection of presidential memorabilia and captivating artworks. Immerse yourself in the meticulously landscaped gardens and catch a glimpse of the public amphitheater that occasionally comes alive with cultural performances.

Insights

Immerse yourself in an adventure through time at The Mansion, where the Philippines' presidential legacy meets architectural magnificence. From its storied history to the meticulously curated mini-museum, this sanctuary provides a tranquil escape for those seeking a blend of heritage and natural beauty.

OUR LADY OF LOURDES GROTTO

What to expect

The Our Lady of Lourdes Grotto is a Catholic shrine that sits on Mirador Hill in Baguio City. It was constructed in 1913 mainly out of limestone that was used to build the Mirador Jesuit Villa. Inside the grotto, you will find the Our Lady of Lourdes statue. This sculpture was created by the famous artist Isabelo Tampingco using polychromed molave. Aside from serving as a place of worship, many tourists flock here for the scenic view of Baguio City that the grotto offers.

What can you do

To reach the shrine, visitors must climb 252 steps or drive a light vehicle up a winding and steep paved road. Light a candle in tradition as you enter the grotto, home to the exquisite polychromed molave statue of Our Lady of Lourdes, a masterpiece by sculptor Isabelo Tampingco. And also, don’t miss the chance to capture the breathtaking cityscape from this elevated vantage point.

Insights

The Our Lady of Lourdes Grotto presents a harmonious blend of spirituality and natural beauty. As visitors ascend the steps or wind their way up the road to this Catholic shrine, they are met with the awe-inspiring Our Lady of Lourdes statue, an artistic masterpiece that stands as a testament to faith. The panoramic view of Baguio City from this elevated spot serves as a profound reminder of the connection between divine devotion and the breathtaking world that surrounds us.

OUR LADY OF ATONEMENT CATHEDRAL

What to expect

The cathedral has a distinct pink facade with a rose window and twin square bell towers with pyramidal roofs. Within its large courtyard is a view deck that overlooks Session Road and the downtown commercial district of Baguio City. The cathedral is accessible to pedestrians from Session Road through a 100-step stone staircase, or through the adjacent campus of Saint Louis University

What can you do

Ascend the iconic 100-step stairway or explore the winding alleys to reach the Baguio Cathedral, a rosy masterpiece in the heart of the city. Inside the Cathedral, you will be drawn by the artistic depiction of religious images and sceneries. The altar has a grandiose feel with religious stained-glass windows embellished with gold trimmings. At the background and on top of the altar prime stained-glass windows encased in a half shell-like dome structure that spells a commanding finishing touch to the whole scene.

Insights

The Baguio Cathedral is a stunning sight that welcomes visitors with its pink walls and towers. It is a symbol of faith and resilience, as it survived the bombings of World War II. The cathedral offers a panoramic view of the city from its courtyard, as well as a glimpse of its rich history and culture from its interior. You will be amazed by the intricate stained-glass windows, the majestic altar, and the serene atmosphere of the cathedral. The Baguio Cathedral is not just a place of worship, but also a place of beauty and wonder.

The Cemetery of Negativism ultimately stands as a powerful metaphor for the transformative journey from negativity to positivity.

Burnham Park

What to expect

The 'mother of all parks' in Baguio city, the summer capital of the Philippines, is a 32.84-hectare park designed by, and named after, American architect and urban planner Daniel Hudson Burnham, who also designed the original plan for Baguio City. The park has 12 main areas, the most popular of which are the Burnham Lagoon, a man-made lake with rowboats for hire; a children's playground; a skating rink; the Rose Garden and the Picnic Grove.

What can you do

At Burnham Park, you can enjoy various activities for all travelers. Jog or walk in nature's embrace. Boat on the calm Burnham Lake. Play at the playground, or picnic in the sun. Bike through the park's beauty, and take amazing photos. Burnham Park is a place to make memorable moments.

Insights

Experience the 'Mother of All Parks' at Burnham Park, Baguio City's most beloved recreational center. With breathtaking views, a man-made lake, a playground, a skating rink, and a picnic grove, there's something for everyone. Whether you're jogging, boating, cycling or simply strolling, Burnham Park is a picturesque memory waiting to be made.

BAGUIO PUBLIC MARKET

What to expect

When going to Baguio Public Market, visitors can expect a clean and organized market where they can shop for fresh produce, meat, seafood, and a wide variety of goods, including clothing, souvenirs, and household items. The market is divided into two sections: the wet market and the dry market. The wet market is where visitors can find fresh produce, meat, fish, and poultry, while the dry market is where they can find various goods.

What can you do

Visitors can do a lot of things in Baguio Public Market. They can buy fresh vegetables, fruits, and strawberries, which are considered a must-buy when visiting Baguio. They can also buy pasalubong treats like lengua de gato, peanut brittle, handicrafts, and souvenirs made by local artisans. Visitors can also go thrift shopping at the ukay-ukay stalls, where they can buy clothes and accessories. Additionally, visitors can explore the Maharlika Livelihood Complex, located along Magsaysay Avenue and recognizable due to its unique Cordillera roof design that’s unlike any other in the area

Insights

Baguio Public Market is a place to buy fresh produce and a one-stop-shop for all your household needs and souvenirs. Visitors can expect a clean and organized market with two sections, the wet market and the dry market, where they can find various goods, including clothing, handicrafts, and pasalubong treats. The market is a must-visit destination in Baguio, where visitors can buy fresh vegetables, fruits, and strawberries, which are considered a must-buy when visiting the city.

NIGHT MARKET

What to expect

Baguio City Night Market is a bustling outdoor market along Harrison Road, offering a unique shopping experience for visitors. From 9 p.m. onwards, the street transforms into a vibrant marketplace with stalls offering various goods, including clothing, crafts, secondhand items, and street food. The market is open till 4 a.m., making it a perfect destination for night owls. The atmosphere is electric, with locals and tourists navigating through the maze of vendors. It's not just a shopping experience; it's a cultural excursion that encapsulates the spirit of Baguio.

What can you do

Visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture and enjoy the thrill of bargain hunting. Baguio Night Market offers a wide range of activities, such as shopping for souvenirs, discovering local handicrafts, sampling street food, shopping for clothes, and finding the perfect pair of shoes. Foodies will enjoy the many food stalls at the Night Market, selling everything from grilled meats to street snacks. Fashionistas can find a wide range of clothes, from T-shirts and shorts to dresses and jeans – both brand new and "ukay." The Night Market also offers a wide selection of shoes, from sandals and flip-flops to sneakers and boots

Insights

A Night to Remember Experience the lively and colorful night market of Baguio, where you can find everything from clothes and crafts to food and fun. Insights
