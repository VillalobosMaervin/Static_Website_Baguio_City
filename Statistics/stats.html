Yearly Tourist Arrivals

At exactly 4:26 p.m. on Monday, 16 July 1990, a killer earthquake unexpectedly hit and extensively devastated the City of Baguio. The powerful temblor measured 7.7 in the open-ended Richter scale and lasted for 45 seconds. It was said to be the most destructive earthquake on record within the Cordillera region. There were numerous aftershocks that followed and the strongest, which occurred at 3:15 a.m. of 18 July 1990, lasted for eight (8) seconds and measured 5.3 on the Richter Scale.

Trend Analysis

Tourism in Baguio experienced consistent growth from 2011 to 2018, with the highest growth rate in 2013 (36.42%). However, in 2019, there was a decline of 13.16%, and in 2020, a significant drop of 82.41% due to external factors such as the COVID-19 pandemic. The year 2022 showed a remarkable recovery with a growth rate of 289.19%.

Impact of External Factors

The tourism industry in Baguio was significantly affected by the COVID-19 pandemic, as seen in the sharp decline in 2020 and the slow recovery in 2021 and 2022.

2011

Baguio Tourism Annual Report (2011): A Promising Growth and Sustainable Development

In 2011, Baguio's tourism sector welcomed 552,497 tourists, marking the beginning of a growth trend. This represented an 11.69% increase from the previous year. The year laid a solid foundation for future growth as efforts to promote Baguio as a tourist destination started yielding positive results. However, sustaining this growth momentum would require continued marketing efforts and investments in infrastructure development and maintenance to enhance the overall tourist experience.

2012

Baguio Tourism Annual Report (2012): Sustaining Growth While Addressing Sustainability Concerns

In 2012, Baguio continued to experience positive growth in its tourism sector, with 617,079 tourist arrivals. This indicated an 11.61% increase from the previous year. The year saw double-digit growth for the second consecutive year, attributed to enhanced marketing efforts and improved accessibility to the city. Positive feedback from tourists regarding Baguio's natural beauty and attractions further fueled the growth. Challenges included the need to maintain this growth while managing its impact on local infrastructure and addressing sustainability concerns.

2013

Baguio Tourism Annual Report (2013): Record-Breaking Year in the Midst of Challenges

The year 2013 was a significant turning point for Baguio's tourism, with 843,473 tourist arrivals, representing a remarkable 36.42% growth from the previous year. Exceptional growth was attributed to effective marketing campaigns, making it a record-breaking year for Baguio. Tourists were drawn to the city's appeal, and positive word-of-mouth further boosted arrivals. Challenges included managing the influx of tourists while ensuring a quality experience and developing infrastructure to keep pace with increasing demand.

2014

Baguio Tourism Annual Report (2014): A Continuous Growth and Development

Tourism in Baguio continued to thrive in 2014, with 932,292 tourist arrivals, indicating a growth rate of 10.54%. The growth trend was sustained, albeit at a slightly lower rate. Baguio's reputation as a top destination for local and international tourists solidified, and investments in infrastructure development contributed to positive experiences. Challenges included ensuring that growth remained sustainable and did not harm the environment, as well as meeting increased demands for accommodations and services

2015-2022

Baguio Tourism Annual Report (2015-2022): A Tale of Resilience and Recovery

During this period, there was notable growth in tourist arrivals, with the peak occurring between 2015 and 2018. However, the industry experienced a significant decline in 2020 due to the pandemic. Remarkably, Baguio saw a strong recovery in 2022, with a remarkable growth rate of 289.19%.

TOURISM DURING THE PANDEMIC IN BAGUIO CITY

2019

Pre-Pandemic

In 2019, Baguio's tourism sector enjoyed a period of stability, welcoming 1,526,458 tourists. The city maintained a consistent flow of visitors, although challenges emerged in balancing growth with sustainability and preserving the high-quality visitor experience that Baguio is known for. Recommendations during this time emphasized the importance of continued investment in infrastructure, environmental conservation efforts, and the exploration of innovative marketing strategies to sustain growth.

2020

During the Pandemic

The year 2020 was a stark contrast as the COVID-19 pandemic led to a significant decline in tourist arrivals, with only 268,192 visitors. This downturn highlighted the vulnerability of the tourism industry to external shocks. The year was characterized by a focus on health and safety measures, government support for affected businesses, and the need for long-term resilience planning to mitigate future disruptions.

2021-2023

Post-Pandemic

Beginning in 2021, Baguio embarked on a journey of recovery as travel restrictions eased and visitor confidence slowly returned. The city welcomed 267,480 tourists in 2021, reflecting ongoing concerns related to the pandemic. However, a remarkable resurgence was observed in 2022, with 1,042,309 tourist arrivals, signaling a remarkable growth rate of 289.19%. Challenges in these post-pandemic years included managing the influx of tourists, infrastructure development, and the pursuit of sustainable growth.
